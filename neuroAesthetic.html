NeuroAesthetic

- Making Art With Your Brain -

Inspired by my Japanese cultural heritage and childhood memories, this project bridges tradition and modernity through an interactive art installation.

I thought, “What if the shoji paper windows could serve as a canvas to display mental states?”

By combining incense as an olfactory guide and EEG technology, it visualizes brainwave patterns on shoji paper windows to create a dynamic and meditative ambiance. The installation fosters mindfulness and introspection, blending tradition, technology, and innovative art.

For this project I worked with my friend Fleuriane LAM, who wanted to create art from brainwaves to promote creativity.
We used the Muse 2 headset, a commercial EEG device designed to track electrical signals generated by brain activity.
This headset captures brainwave data, enabling the real-time monitoring and analysis of mental states such as relaxation, focus, and mindfulness, which serve as the foundation for creating interactive and immersive visualizations in the installation.

By connecting the Muse 2 headset to a computer, we were able to collect raw, unfiltered electrical signals generated by brain activity.
Using Python, we processed these signals and filtered them into five distinct brainwave types based on their frequency ranges:
- Delta (0.5 - 4 Hz), associated with deep relaxation and sleep;
- Theta (4 - 8 Hz), linked to creativity and meditation;
- Alpha (8 - 12 Hz), representing calmness and readiness;
- Beta (12 - 30 Hz), connected to active thinking and focus;
- Gamma (30 - 100 Hz), associated with cognitive functions and heightened awareness.

We sent the filtered data via Open Sound Control (OSC) on port 5000, which was then received by TouchDesigner—a node-based visual programming software used for real-time graphics, interactive media, and data visualization.
Once the data was successfully transmitted, we had to first become familiar with TouchDesigner's interface and workflow.
This allowed us to create visuals that respond dynamically to the EEG sensor data, enabling real-time interaction and artistic expression.
