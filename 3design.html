Honda HA-420

- Flying with renewable fuel -

The goal of this project was to model an existing aircraft using SolidWorks with real-scale measurements. I chose the HondaJet HA-420 for this project, as it was the first aircraft to fly using renewable fuel derived from the microalga Euglena, in collaboration with Susteo.

Susteo is a subsidiary of Euglena Co., Ltd., a Japanese biotechnology company specializing in the development of sustainable biofuels. Their work focuses on producing next-generation aviation fuels from Euglena microalgae, which have the potential to reduce carbon emissions compared to conventional fossil fuels. In December 2021, Honda Aircraft Company successfully conducted a test flight of the HondaJet HA-420 using Susteo's biofuel, marking a significant step in the transition toward greener aviation.

One of the main difficulties we encountered was finding the blueprints or detailed schematics of the aircraft to make our HondaJet model as realistic as possible. The only reference materials we were able to find were simplified schematics and patterns intended for paper plane models, which lacked the precision and detail required for an accurate 3D reconstruction.

For my part, I primarily focused on modeling the main body and wings of the jet, while other team members worked on designing the tires and engines.
Since some of the necessary information was unavailable, I had to make certain approximations to ensure the model remained as accurate and proportionate as possible.

The final step was to assemble all the individual parts onto the main body. To achieve a complete 3D model, we used the symmetry function, which allowed us to accurately replicate and align components for a balanced and realistic representation of the aircraft.
